The World of Soccer

Spotlight: The Legend of Pelé

Edson Arantes do Nascimento, known globally as Pelé, is widely regarded as one of the greatest athletes to ever play the game. He is the only player to have won three FIFA World Cups, and his incredible skill helped popularize the sport in the United States. His legacy continues to inspire millions of young players to pick up a ball for the first time.

A Brief History of the Beautiful Game

  • 1863: The Football Association is formed in England, establishing the first official rules.
  • 1930: The first-ever FIFA World Cup is hosted and won by Uruguay.
  • 1991: The inaugural Women's World Cup takes place in China.

I first started playing soccer in a local youth league, where I learned the importance of dedication and sportsmanship. What interests me most about this sport is how it acts as a "universal language"—no matter where you are in the world, you can start a game with nothing more than a ball and two markers for goals.
